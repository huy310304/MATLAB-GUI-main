Instructions for Using MATLAB App

  • Generate Matrix

    - Input the number of rows and columns in the respective fields.
    - Click Generate Matrix to create a matrix filled with zeros.
    - You can manually input values into the matrix using the table.

  • RREF (Reduced Row Echelon Form)

    - After generating the matrix, click RREF to compute the Reduced Row Echelon Form.
    - Ensure that the matrix is numeric and contains valid entries.

  • Solve Augmented Matrix

    - Enter your matrix on the left and the vector b on the right.
    - Click Solve Augmented Matrix to solve the system of linear equations.
    - Ensure that the augmented matrix does not contain NaN values.

  • Check Span

    - Input your matrix and vector b.
    - Click Check Span to check if the vector b is within the span of the matrix.
    - The result will appear in the Results field below.

  • Check Linearly Independent

    - Input your matrix.
    - Click Check Linearly Independent to determine whether the set of vectors (columns) is linearly independent.

  • Find Row Space Basis

    - Enter your matrix and click Find Row Space Basis to compute the basis of the row space.

  • Find Column Space Basis

    - Enter your matrix and click Find Column Space Basis to compute the basis of the column space.

  • Plot Column Vectors

    - Enter your matrix.
    - Click Plot Column Vectors to visualize the column vectors in 3D space.
    - Ensure that the matrix has valid 3D vectors.

  • Plot Single Vector

    - Enter a single vector in the matrix or vector b field.
    - Click Plot Single Vector to visualize the vector in the 3D plot.

  • Find Null Space Basis

    - Enter your matrix and click Find Null Space Basis to compute the basis of the null space.

  • Find Rank

    - Enter your matrix and click Find Rank to compute the rank of the matrix.

  • Find Nullity

    - Enter your matrix and click Find Nullity to compute the nullity of the matrix (dimension of the null space).

  • Swap Row

    - Enter the indices of two rows in the RowSwap1 and RowSwap2 fields.
    - Click Swap Row to swap the two rows in the matrix.

  • Multiply Row

    - Enter the index of the row to be multiplied and the scalar value in the respective fields.
    - Click Multiply Row to multiply the row by the scalar factor.

  • Add Multiple of Source Row to Destination Row

    - Enter the source row, destination row, and the multiplier in the respective fields.
    - Click Add Multiple of Source Row to Destination Row to perform the row operation.

  • Determinant

    - Enter a square matrix.
    - Click Determinant to compute the determinant of the matrix.
    - Ensure the matrix is square (same number of rows and columns).
